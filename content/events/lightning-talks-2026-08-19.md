PyLadies Vancouver and **R-Ladies Vancouver** are teaming up for a night of **lightning
talks**: short talks from speakers in both communities. Come for a fast, friendly mix of
Python and R, ideas, tools, and stories, all in bite-sized form. We'll be at **VISST**
from **6 to 8 PM**.

## We're looking for speakers 🎤

This is a call for lightning talks, and we'd love to hear from you. We're looking for
short presentations, **5 to 15 minutes** long, from speakers in either community, PyLadies
or R-Ladies. It's the lowest-pressure way to get on stage.

- **Beginners are especially encouraged.** A lightning talk is a great first talk, and a
  great way to practise before you present at a conference.
- **Yes, you can reuse a talk you've already given elsewhere.** New members may not have
  heard it, so a talk from another meetup, conference, or class is welcome here.
- **Python or R, either is welcome**, and technology topics adjacent to them. When we
  polled our last meetup, people asked for AI/ML and LLMs, web development, data science,
  Python internals, testing and tooling, and live coding.

New to the format, unsure what to talk about, or want examples to watch? Our
**[Lightning Talks guide](/lightning-talks/)** covers what a lightning talk is, how to
prepare, topic ideas, example talks to watch, and common questions.

## Details

- **When:** Wednesday, August 19, 2026, 6 to 8 PM
- **Where:** VISST, 1490 West Broadway, Vancouver
- **Who:** Everyone is welcome. This is a joint event with R-Ladies Vancouver.

If this is your first R-Ladies event, please take a moment to review the
[R-Ladies Global code of conduct](https://github.com/rladies/starter-kit/wiki/Code-of-Conduct).
